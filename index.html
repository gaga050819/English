Taras Shevchenko

The spiritual father of modern Ukraine, a visionary poet, and a master artist whose words and images ignited the fight for freedom.

The Legacy

Born on March 9, 1814, in the village of Moryntsi, Taras Shevchenko was destined for a life of hardship as a serf. Orphaned at a young age, he nevertheless harbored an unyielding passion for art and poetry.

His exceptional talent was noticed by prominent artists while he was in Saint Petersburg with his owner. In a defining moment for Ukrainian culture, his friends organized a lottery to purchase his freedom from serfdom in 1838. This liberated him to study at the Academy of Arts, where his genius fully blossomed.

Shevchenko's literary masterpiece, "Kobzar" (1840), became the foundation of modern Ukrainian literature. His powerful verses championed freedom and love for his homeland, often criticizing the Russian Empire. For his revolutionary ideas, he was arrested and condemned to years of exile with a ban on writing and painting. Yet, he continued to create in secret, becoming an immortal symbol of unbreakable spirit. Today, he is revered as Ukraine's national hero and prophet.

Life Journey

1814
Born into serfdom in Moryntsi, Kyiv Governorate.
1838
Freed from serfdom; enters the Imperial Academy of Arts.
1840
Publishes "Kobzar", his seminal collection of poetry.
1847
Arrested for political activities and sent into military exile.
1861
Passes away in Saint Petersburg, just days before the abolition of serfdom. Reburied in Kaniv, Ukraine.
